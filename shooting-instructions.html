Helmet Mounted Camera

The most important thing to do when shooting facial performance for the DDH pipline is to have enough distance between the face and the camera. Because this process relies on undistorted facial video the center of the image sensor is where you need to have the face.

1. Follow FW instructions on setting up the Mark 4 camera

i. Set camera to 24 fps (ideal framerate)

ii. Use the 4.3mm lens

iii. Frame up your subject's face so the eyes and mouth are safely in center of the lens the blue square reference as indicated below

IMAGE HERE

Everyone's face is different, to achieve the perfect centered framing of the face we find 8” to 10” rods are required.

IMAGE HERE

2. Lighting

You will need to light your subject as flat as possible to avoid “baking” any lighting into the subject. We found a ring light and reflector cards mounted to the side of the helmet worked best. It is important not not have any light casting shadows on the face as the performer moves about
